New mother Deanna Rose Gioia suddenly dies while feeding her baby

April 28, 2024
blog

A family has been left devastated after a beloved new mother suddenly died while she held her baby girls in her arms. 

Deanna Rose Gioia, a popular 35-year-old Staten Island nurse, was cradling two-month-old Adrianna as she prepared to feed her on Sunday. 

Suddenly, she called out to her husband Richard Gioia: 'I don't feel well'. 

She was already unresponsive when he ran into the room mere seconds later. An ambulance took Deanna to a nearby hospital, where she was pronounced dead.

Deanna Rose Goia, 35, was holding her baby girl Adrianna (pictured together with husband Richard) in her arms on Sunday when she suddenly died 

Deanna and Richard had welcomed their baby girl to the world just two months before. Friends said Deanna was in good health and had no complications during the birth 

'Just like that, she was gone,' Debbie DiSalvatore, Deanna's mother, told Staten Island Live. 

The cause of Deanna's death still remains a mystery.

She was in good health and didn't have any complications when she gave birth. Deanna died just two days after her daughter turned two months old. 

As her family awaits the autopsy report to reveal Deanna's cause of death, they are only beginning to come to terms with their sudden loss. 

The cause of Deanna's death still remains a mystery as her family awaits the autopsy

Deanna's mother said she was a natural nurse, one who has been taking care of people her whole life. 

When DiSalvatore's father was dying, it was Deanna who helped take care of him and helped her mother make decisions regarding the end of his life. 

Deanna put off college to figure out what she wanted to do with her life, and she found her answer at Staten Island University Hospital North. 

She began working as a patient liaison in the hospital emergency department, and soon decided to go to nursing school. 

'She saw that the ER is like a family and the care they give is incredible,' Deanna's friend, Michelle Paini, told Staten Island Live. 

'She saw staff there - all the doctors, all the nurse - what they did for people, and she wanted to do that.' 

Deanna became a registered nurse in 2012 and loved her job and patients, friends said. 

But it was her love for husband Richard that family said was the kind you find in storybooks.

Deanna and Richard while they both worked part-time in a supermarket as teenagers.

Their paths crossed again nine years later at a cafe and the couple became inseparable, tying the knot in March 2011. 

Deanna's mother said she had never seen two people so in love.  

'He was her prince charming,' DiSalvatore said. 'He would do anything for her. He made her life perfect' 

'A love like that you don't see between two people. Everyone who was at their wedding said that to me.'  

Deanna showed the strength of the couple's union in her very own daughter's name, combing the 'Anna' with the 'Ri' from Richard.

Adrianna Rose's birth on May 29 was the happiest day of Deanna's life, her mother said. 

'She adored Adrianna,' DiSalvatore added. 'Deanna was such a good mother.' 

Now friends and family are rallying together for the little girl who lost her mother too soon - and too suddenly. 

A GoFundMe page has since raised more than $60,000 for the newborn. Nearly $43,000 was raised in just 24 hours. 

'The world lost an amazing woman that made everyone she was around better,' the page begins. 

'Deanna was a nurse both inside and outside of the hospital, always helping anyone in need. She was the friend who held the group together, always having everyone's back.'

Early Life

Eric Cole was born on 12th June 1988 in Palm Springs, California, United States. His father’s name is Bobby Cole and played on the PGA Champions Tour from 1998 to 2001. At the age of 18, his father won the British Amateur at Carnoustie (1966).

His mother’s name is Laura Baugh, a former LPGA star. At the time, his mother was the youngest U.S. Women’s Amateur winner and turned Pro in 1973. His parent’s married in 1980 and enjoyed success at different stages of their careers. Together, his parents had seven children, including Eric who is aware of his parent’s golf legacy.

Talking about Eric’s Education, he earned a primary school education. Later, he graduated from Nova Southeastern University. During his college days, he faced the curious loss of weight. Later, he was diagnosed with Type 1 diabetes. Later, he also earned professional golf training in the United States. 

Eric Cole Career

Eric started his career path as a Golf player. While studying in college, he was interested in sports activities like basketball, but he decided to pursue golf. He practiced and played his golf for a junior rate at West Orange Country Club in Winter Garden. He turned Pro in 2009 and played at various local events, mini-tours, and Q-school in the United States.

In 2016, he earned a Korn Ferry Tour membership but did not retain his status. In 2019, he won 14 times on the minor association golf tour. He qualified for the 2021 U.S. Open at Torrey Pines Golf Course, where he missed the cut after rounds of 77–73.

In 2020, he regained Korn Ferry Tour status via Q-school and earned his first PGA Tour card in 2022. In 2022, he prepared for the 2022-23 PGA tour season in Napa, California. At the Honda Classic, he came closer to sealing the victory but in the end, he was defeated by Chris Kirk.

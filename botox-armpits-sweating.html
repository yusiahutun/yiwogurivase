Why Chrissy Teigen has Botox in her armpits

February 17, 2024
blog

This week, Chrissy Teigen documented how she has Botox injections in her armpits via Instagram Stories. “Botoxed my armpits. Truly best move I have ever made,” she captioned her video of the experience. “I can wear silk again without soaking,” the model and presenter added.

This procedure is commonly used to treat hyperhidrosis, more commonly known as excessive sweating, which is said to affect 7.5 million people worldwide. Whether the number is rising remains to be seen, but there has been a 32 per cent increase in Google searches for ‘excessive sweating’ in 2019.

Botox injections for hyperhidrosis

Aesthetician Dr Esho, who has clinics in London, Newcastle and Dubai, explains: "Normally, your nervous system activates your sweat glands when your body temperature rises, and this is how your body automatically cools itself. In people with hyperhidrosis, however, the nerves that signal the sweat glands are overactive.” Botox treats hyperhidrosis by blocking the nerves responsible for activating your sweat glands.

"When you receive Botox injections directly into the area of your body that commonly sweats, your overactive nerves are essentially paralysed. When your nerves can’t signal to your sweat glands, you don’t sweat.” Botox only prevents sweating in the specific area where it’s injected, so, used in this way, the procedure can also be performed on the hands and feet.

"Studies have found that Botox successfully treats sweaty palms in 80-to-90 per cent of cases,” Dr Esho notes. “However, the treatments don’t last quite as long as it does with the underarm area, which can last for at least six months.” Studies also show that Botox works to treat forehead sweat. “It can reduce sweating by 75 per cent for about five months,” the doctor confirms.

Dr David Eccleston, the clinical director of MediZen in Birmingham who has over 20 years’ experience in the management of underarm sweating, reassures that using Botox in this way is “extremely safe when performed correctly by a medically qualified practitioner”. He explains, “the dose used is very low, and the Botox is rapidly taken up into the nerve endings after injection, so there is no spread to other areas.”

MiraDry treatment for hyperhidrosis

Those wanting a permeant treatment for hyperhidrosis should look to the miraDry radiofrequency system, “which permanently destroys the sweat glands in the armpits after one or sometimes two treatments,” Dr Eccleston explains.

FDA-approved, “miraDry uses thermal energy which is delivered into the underarm region using a specially designed hand-piece,” Dr Esho reveals. “The energy is carefully targeted to the sweat glands in that area and destroys them via thermolysis. The procedure gives a long-lasting reduction in sweat.” It works immediately, and is non-invasive – “meaning minimal downtime,” the doctor adds. Just expect a little bruising.

Dr Tapan Patel, a renowned aesthetic physician, and director of the PHI Clinic in London, has been using the miraDry technology for nearly six years. He feels, “in terms of our patient satisfaction there is nothing better on the market” for treating excessive sweating.

As to whether it’s unhealthy to go without our underarm sweat glands, the makers of miraDry say no. Given that the body has two-to-four million sweat glands, only about two per cent are found in the underarms. “Unlike some procedures used to treat excessive sweating, there is no demonstrable increase in sweating in other areas,” Dr Esho explains.

The cost for miraDry is around £1650, compared to £550 for Botox injections. For something much more affordable, but entirely temporary, speak to your GP about prescriptive deodorants.

"Prescription antiperspirant has a strong concentration of metallic salts and should only be considered after exhausting all other over-the-counter methods to reduce excessive sweating," Dr Esho explains.

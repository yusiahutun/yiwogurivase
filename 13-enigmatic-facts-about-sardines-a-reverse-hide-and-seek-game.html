FAQs

1. Are sardines actually a type of fish?

Yes, sardines are a specific type of small, oily fish. They belong to the herring family and are known for their distinct flavor and rich nutritional profile.

2. Are sardines only found in specific regions?

No, sardines are found in various oceans around the world. They are particularly abundant in the Mediterranean Sea and the coasts of Portugal, Spain, and Morocco.

3. Are sardines good for your health?

Absolutely! Sardines are packed with omega-3 fatty acids, vitamins, and minerals that support heart health, brain function, and bone strength. They are also a great source of protein.

4. Can sardines be eaten straight from the can?

Yes, sardines can be enjoyed straight from the can. They are often preserved in oil or salt and can be eaten as a convenient and nutritious snack.

5. Do sardines have any environmental impact?

While sardines are a sustainable choice compared to some other fish species, it’s important to ensure that they are harvested responsibly to prevent overfishing and maintain ecosystem balance.

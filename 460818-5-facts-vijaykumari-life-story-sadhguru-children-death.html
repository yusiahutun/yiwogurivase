5 facts about Vijaykumari: Life story, Sadhguru, children, cause of death

April 26, 2024

In the Hindu culture, it is widely accepted that a person can leave their body willingly without damaging it through an act called Mahasamadhi. Since time immemorial, many sages and saints have taken Mahasamadhi. Vijaykumari took Mahasamadhi on 23 January 1997, and her connection to the material world ceased to exist. However, her death became a controversial subject six months later. The public became interested in the case, especially after the police investigated her husband, Sadhguru, for murder.

Vijaykumari was an Indian yogi teacher, banker, and volunteer. She was well known as the wife of Jagadish Vasudev, better known as Sadhguru. Furthermore, she was the mother of Radhe Jaggi, a now-famous Indian classical dancer.

Despite her death in 1997, many people still question her cause of death. But that has to do with her association with Sadhguru, the founder of the Isha Foundation and one of the most renowned spiritual leaders in the world today.

Top facts about Vijaykumari

One might argue that Vijaykumari died early and may not have achieved her ambition. However, she lived a life of devotion and interacted with people with respect. But are there some unknown facts about her?

1. She married Sadhguru in 1984

The former banker met Sadhguru in Mysore, a city in the southern part of the state of Karnataka, India, in the early 1980s. The two met at a lunch where a friend had invited Sadhguru. After their first meeting, Sadhguru and Vijaykumari started dating. They exchanged heartfelt letters for a short period until 1984.

In 1984, their relationship culminated in marriage. However, the couple's marriage ceremony occurred on 20 May 1985 in Tiruppur. They did their wedding on the auspicious occasion of Mahashivratri, an annual festival dedicated to the Hindu god Shiva.

2. Vijaykumari's death raised concerns several months later

On 23 January 1997, the former banker attained Mahasamadhi. Mahasamadhi is a state a yogi enters when they consciously decide to leave their body. However, that only happens when the yogi has already achieved God's realisation or nirvikalpa Samadhi. Thus, the yogi recognises and experiences their true oneness and unity with God.

After her death, Vijaykumari’s husband cremated her 12 hours later, at 6.00 am. However, her death raised questions seven months later. Her father filed a First Information Report at the Coimbatore Police Station accusing Sadhguru of murdering her daughter, mainly because her cremation happened fast. The police started their investigations.

They spent eight months looking into the allegations and even questioned Sadhguru about the events that led to his wife’s death. Before her death, a doctor had examined her and claimed she had died of a heart attack. However, no doctor performed an autopsy on her.

The doctor claimed that the cause of the heart attack could have been a result of stress or strain, an accident, heart disease, or a snake bite. Also, he ruled out hom**icide, sui*cide*, and poisoning. It took seven more months of investigations into the death of Vijji.

The police collected some of her ashes and bones and tested them forensically. However, they found no traces of poisoning. Although Sadhguru was still under investigation, the police could not get a conviction. Then, on 8 January 1999, the court dropped the case citing a lack of evidence.

Vijaykumari and Sadhguru were married for six years and welcomed their first child, Radhe Jaggi, in 1990 (age 32 as of 2022). Before her birth, she was always keen on having a child. Vijji felt that motherhood was a crucial experience in every woman’s life.

The personality died when her daughter was seven years old. Today, Radhe is a dancer, yoga teacher, and social media influencer. She studied at Rishi Valley School for eight years and spent four years learning Bharathanatiyam, an Indian classical dance, at Kalakshetra. Radhe’s father always wanted her daughter to be a dancer years before she was born.

He had the thought after visiting Kalakshetra with his wife in the late 1980s. In 2014, when Radhe was 24, she married her husband, Sandeep Narayanan. The wedding ceremony happened at her father’s Isha Foundation.

4. She graduated with a degree in commerce

What did Vijaykumari’s study in college? She had studied and graduated with a degree in commerce. She worked as a clerk at the State Bank of India in Bangalore, India. After marrying Sadhguru, she became a yoga teacher in Rishi Probhajar’s SSY.

Vijji operated as a teacher under SSY for several years until 1992, when her husband founded the Isha Foundation, where she continued her work as a yogi teacher. Furthermore, she would accompany her husband on his motorcycle to volunteer in some of his teaching programmes.

5. Vijji helped her husband find the Isha Foundation

Vijaykumari supported her husband throughout their early years of marriage in his work as a yogi teacher. In 1992, he founded the Isha Foundation, a non-profit spiritual organisation in Coimbatore, India. The foundation hosts the Isha Yoga Centre, which they formed in 1994. It offers yoga programmes intended to introduce a sense of compassion and inclusiveness.

The Isha Foundation has expanded internationally, including conducting yoga programmes in the United States as early as 1997. In 2020, the Foundation’s US chapter collected revenues of $25 million. Its revenue sources are donations from wellwishers, the sale of spiritual books, and charges for yoga classes.

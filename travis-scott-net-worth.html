Music Career

Travis Scott has established himself as a versatile and prominent artist through a succession of impactful albums and collaborations. His first solo full-length record, the mixtape “Owl Pharaoh,” was published on iTunes in May 2013 after sample clearance concerns delayed it. This preceded his second mixtape, “Days Before Rodeo,” released in August 2014, highlighting Scott’s musical evolution.

He broke through with his debut studio album, “Rodeo,” released in September 2015, featuring Quavo, Juicy J, Kanye West, The Weeknd, Swae Lee, and Justin Bieber. A critical and economic triumph, “Rodeo” opened at number three on the US Billboard 200.

In September 2016, Scott’s second studio album, “Birds in the Trap Sing McKnight,” reached number one on the US Billboard 200. In addition, he acquired a global partnership with Universal Music Publishing Group, boosting his impact.

In December 2017, Scott released “Huncho Jack, Jack Huncho” with Quavo, continuing his collaborations. His third solo album, “Astroworld,” released in August 2018, became a global hit. The album’s second song, “Sicko Mode,” topped the Billboard Hot 100.

On a bright note, Scott released “JackBoys” from Cactus Jack to end 2019. “Pick Up The Phone,” “Portland,” “Deserve,” “Watch,” and “Power is Power,” showed his versatility and impact across genres.

Travis Scott’s production skills have shaped the industry beyond his solo career. He executive produced Kanye West’s “Cruel Winter” and produced Rihanna’s “Bitch Better Have My Money.”

He won the 2016 BET Hip Hop Awards People’s Champ Award and the 2019 Billboard Music Awards Top Streaming Song (Audio) for “Sicko Mode”. Grammy nominations, including Album of the Year for Justin Bieber’s “Purpose” in 2017, demonstrate his influence on music.

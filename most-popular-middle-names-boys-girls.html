These are the most popular middle names in the UK

April 21, 2024
blog

Getty

When it comes to choosing middle names, parents are much less likely to opt for an unusual moniker than they might do when picking a first name, a new study has confirmed. According to Ancestry UK, the most popular middle names for both boys and girls are those that have been around for a long, long time.

Louise, Rose and Grace unsurprisingly take the top spots for girls, while James, John and William are the most popular middle names for boys in the UK.

Although monikers such as Thor, Atlas and Ocean are predicted to be big in 2017, we're less likely to see this trend hitting middle names. The reason for this is that they are a fairly new invention, driven simply by the desire to commemorate ancestors.

"Middle names are less likely to follow popular culture and more likely to reflect age-old traditions or names that were popular in our parents' or grandparents' generation – hence the very traditional make-up of today's top 10 middle names," Miriam Silverman at Ancestry UK said.

This can even be seen in A-list circles. The Beckhams – who are well known for picking unusual monikers for their children – do follow this pattern by choosing more traditional middle names with Brooklyn Joseph, Romeo James and Cruz David. However, Harper Seven somewhat bucks the trend.

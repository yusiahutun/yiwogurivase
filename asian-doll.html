Childhood

Asian Doll was born on Dec 7,1996 to Lakeithia Lewis and Michael Allen, second daughter of four children. Allen’s early life was dealt of instability; from poverty (which is like punishment for a crime we didn’t commit) and her father being in and out of prison. She was a member of the Crips (Gang based in the coastal regions of Southern California). Allen attended Skyline High School (Public Magnet School) and dropped out in the 12th grade to follow her passion for rap music and decision paid off for her, as she now has various singles and EPs, millions of fans and followers on social media and a business of her own.

Relationship/Affairs

Asian Doll is currently single, but she has had some relationships in the past. Her most talked-about relationship was the one she had with the late artist King Von.

Asian Doll and King Von relationship began in 2018 till they reportedly broke up in August 2020. They were rumoured to have gotten back together in October 2020, but Von shared a post on Instagram stating that he was still single. Von died in November 2020 during a shooting that occurred in the early hours of November 6 in Atlanta. He was one of the three men who died on November 6, 2020, after he was shot when a shootout ensued at the Monaco Hookah Lounge in Atlanta. Doll took to social media the day he died to mourn the death of her beloved ex. Before her relationship with king Von, Asian Doll dated artist Bandhunta Izzy from April to July 2017.

Public image

In an attempt to distance herself from a group of female rappers using the stage name Doll, Allen changed her name to Asian Da Brat in 2019. The name Asian Da Brat was supposedly a reference to the veteran female rapper from Chicago, Da Brat, whose debut studio album Funkdafied was the first platinum-certified record by a female rapper. In an April 2019 interview on Hot97, Allen stated that Da Brat was a reference to the brand of girls’ toys known as Bratz Dolls. Asian Da Brat has a tattoo of her late boyfriend King Von (who died in 2020) on her left hand.

In January 2021, Allen opened up about suffering depression due to the death of King Von. A few days later, she appeared on an episode of Taraji P. Henson’s show “Peace of Mind with Taraji”.

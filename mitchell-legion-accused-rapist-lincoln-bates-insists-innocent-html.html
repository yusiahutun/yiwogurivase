One of the six South Dakota baseball players charged with rape of a minor boy says he does not fear prison because he is confident he will be acquitted.

In an exclusive interview outside his home in Mitchell, 18-year-old Lincoln Bates told DailyMail.com: 'I am innocent of the charges against me. The charges the DA filed against me are bogus, they are false.'

He went on to deny that he participated in any activity that led to the charges of second-degree rape and aiding and abetting second-degree rape.

Bates is one of a half-dozen baseball players from the Mitchell Post 18 American Legion team who are charged with raping two of their younger teammates in a brutal hazing ritual.

The assault is said to have taken place between June 1 and 4 while they were playing at a tournament in Rapid City, South Dakota, 275 miles from Mitchell.

Mitchell Legion Baseball player Lincoln Bates, 18, spoke to DailyMail.com after he and five of his teammates pleaded not guilty to rape charges involving two minors on their team 

Lincoln Bates, 18, was charged with second-degree rape and aiding and abetting second-degree rape of one 16-year-old boy. He told DailyMail.com he is confident he would be found 'not guilty' if the case goes to trial

The others accused are Hudson Haley, 18, Carter Miller, 18, Landon Waddell, 19, Peyton Mandel, 17, and Karter Sibson, 17.

They all appeared in a Pennington County courtroom last week and pleaded not guilty to their charges. 

While four of the defendants are facing up to 50 years in prison if convicted, Haley and Waddell could be jailed for up to 150 years as they are accused of sexually assaulting a second victim.

The two alleged victims are 16-year-old boys on the team. 

In South Dakota second degree rape is defined as an act of sexual penetration with any person through the use of force, coercion, or threats of immediate and great bodily injury.

When asked if there was any penetration Bates said: 'There was no penetration. No-one was anally raped. They are false charges.'

When pressed further on the events that led the charges, he replied: 'None of your business.' 

From left to right, top: Landon Waddell, 19, Peyton Mandel, 17, Carter Miller, 18. Bottom: Lincoln Bates, 18, Karter Sibson, 17, and Hudson Haley, 18. Landon and Hudson are the only two charged with raping two victims 

He was then asked if the two victims lied to the grand jury and added: 'There was no penetration.'

Bates said he had no worries about a potential prison sentence because if the case went to a trial, he 'would be found not guilty.'

Family members of the alleged victims told DailyMail.com the four teens had digitally raped the younger boys.

Some have characterized the incident as a hazing or initiation ritual gone too far, but Pennington County State's Attorney Lara Roetzel vehemently disagrees with characterizing the incident as such. 

'There are two named victims in this case that reported an incident that happened in Rapid City. It led to the indictments that you see,' she told South Dakota Public Radio before a gag order was imposed at the weekend. 

'Although the case has been characterized as a hazing, or an initiation, that's not what the case is at all. 

'It's disturbing to me that it's being characterized that way. It's a sexual assault case and we need to stop calling it anything other than that,' said Roetzel.

'It just so minimizes and undermines the situation that we have here, which is a very serious criminal offense involving two sexual assaults. And not just sexual assault, but a forcible sexual assault.'

Haley, who is accused of raping both of the victims and is facing decades behind bars if convicted of the charges, has the solid support of his parents.

His mother exclusively told DailyMail.com: 'I would love to talk to you about this case, but I can't due to the gag order.

'Keep pursuing it, there are a lot of interesting things going on here,' she added.

His father Michael Haley, a surgeon, told DailyMail.com: 'God always sheds light on the truth. Only one side of the story is being told.

'We are relying on God and that is where the truth will be revealed. The truth will set these boys free.'

Waddell's father Blake told DailyMail.com: 'At this point the facts are probably one-sided and when other facts come out the truth will be known. The truth will come out throughout the process.

'Unfortunately, in this society right now you are guilty until proven innocent instead of the other way around.'

When asked to further elaborate on what happened that led to his son being charged with rape, 'he said no comment.'

Carter Miller's father, Douglas said: 'The information keeps piling out there and it's all wrong.

'I'm tired of this crap.'

When given a chance to have his son or himself tell their side of the story and set the record straight, he refused any further comment.

The baseball team's coach Luke Norden declined to comment on the allegations

The baseball team's coach Luke Norden would not comment when asked about the accusations.

Classmates of the accused at Mitchell High School said students there are embarrassed by these six boys and their actions. 

'They all come from wealthy and influential families in the area, some of their parents are in the upper-class echelon here in Mitchell,' said one.

Another resident of Mitchell who did not want to be named said: 'The accused families have power and influence here, but where the crime allegedly took place is in Rapid City, 275 miles away, where they are unknown.

'If they are found guilty, they should all serve several years in prison.'

DailyMail.com knows the names of the two 16-year-old victims – identified in court documents as T.S. and E.S. – and spoke with several people associated with them. 

A friend, said: 'The boys are surviving. As you can imagine they are embarrassed and confused about the entire situation.

'They are teens they do not have the mental ability right now to process what is happening, at time they feel like they are to blame, even though they are clearly the victim.

'These young men were assaulted by people they looked up to, their own teammates.

'Some days are better than others, their families are trying to keep their circle very tight. It is a small town of 15,000 people most people know each other and word travels fast. Their families are taking things day by day.'

A status hearing on the case is scheduled for 2:30pm on February 6, 2024.

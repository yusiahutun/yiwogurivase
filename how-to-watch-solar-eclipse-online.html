Everyone across the nation is gearing up for the total solar eclipse on April 8. It will cross North America, passing over Mexico, United States, and Canada, according to NASA’s website, which describes the event as “when Moon passes between the Sun and Earth, completely blocking the face of the Sun. The sky will darken as if it were dawn or dusk.”

To view the solar eclipse in person, you’ll need a special-purpose solar filter to cover your eyes for optimal protection. But if you’d prefer to watch the eclipse from indoors and avoid the crowds, or aren’t residing in one of the 15 states it will be completely visible from—NASA says Texas, Oklahoma, Arkansas, Missouri, Illinois, Kentucky, Indiana, Ohio, Pennsylvania, New York, Vermont, New Hampshire and Maine plus small parts of Tennessee and Michigan will see it in its totality—the event will be live streamed online for all to see.

Early life

Bruno Mars real name is Peter Gene Hernandez. He grew up in a musical family. Bruno Mars father, Pete, was a Latin percussionist from Brooklyn, and his mother, Bernadette was a singer. Mars received his nickname, "Bruno," while he was still a baby.

In Waikiki Beach, his family performed a Las Vegas-style revue that included Motown hits, doo-wop melodies, and celebrity impersonations. Growing up around entertainers, the star began picking up musical instruments from early childhood.

At the age of 4, he joined the family musical act as an Elvis impersonator and quickly became one of the show's stars. He continued to perform with his family throughout his childhood, and as he approached adolescence, he added Michael Jackson to his impersonation repertoire.

The talented musician went to Roosevelt High School, where he and a few friends formed a band, known as the School Boys. They performed classic oldies hits alongside his family's act at the Ilikai Hotel in Honolulu.

Professional career

The star started by being a songwriter. His first song was 'Lost,' which sold for $20,000. He went ahead to be signed to Atlantic Records, where he wrote songs for Flo Rida, Adam Levine, Brandy, among others. He also did some collaboration with BOB in their songs Billionaire and Nothing for you.

In 2010, Bruno had his breakthrough when he released "Just the Way You Are." This was the lead single to his debut studio album Doo-Wops & Hooligans. The album sold over six million copies and debuted to number three on Billboard.

In 2012, he released his second album, Unorthodox Jukebox. The album peaked up to number one on Billboard 200. It also charted as number one in many countries selling six million copies globally.

His third album, titled 24K Magic, was released in 2016. It peaked up to position two on Billboard 200. Some of the songs that did well on the chart are, 'That's what I like,' 'Versace on the Floor,' '24K Magic' among others

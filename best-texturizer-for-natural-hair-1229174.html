    How to Choose the Best Texturizer for Natural Hair?

    Here are a few factors to consider before selecting and purchasing the best texturizer for natural hair.

    • Key Ingredients: Natural hair requires moisturizing and volumizing ingredients in its texturizers to get versatile hairstyles. Nourishing ingredients like rosemary oil and argan oil, herbal plants, essential oils, and hair moisturizing agents should be a must in your list. Similarly, stay clear of harsh chemicals, SLS, parabens, and allergenic products that could damage the hair follicles and irritate the scalp. 
      Our contributor William Slator shares, “Having volumizing ingredients in texturizers is different from using volumizers. While texturizers with volumizing ingredients emphasize on creating texture and smooth movement, ideal for making hairstyles, volumizers focus on increasing overall hair volume.” Keep this point in mind, when choosing a hair texturizer.
    • Hair Texture: Consider the level of curl definition and retention you desire. If looking for poker straight strands try opting for paste and pomades that settle down the frizz and flyaway hair. Some texturizers provide a softening effect that enhances natural curls, while others offer more significant curl reduction. Keep in mind, opting for volumizing sprays can improve the look of thin curls seeking better curl definition. Considerably, natural hair can range from tightly coiled to loose curls, and different texturizers work better with certain hair types. So choose your texturizer wisely. 
    • Results: After finding the ideal hair texturizer, remember these texturizers should not only enhance curls but also provide nourishment and moisture to prevent dryness and breakage. It is important to take into account your overall hair healthiness when applying new hair styling products. 

    Texturizing your hair at home may achieve the same level of customization and precision as the salon. And using the best texturizers for natural hair reduces any hair problems that could come with chemical treatments. Unlike permanent chemical treatments, these hair texturizers will last for 8 to 10 days but keep your hair soft and protected. The products should be chosen based on your hair needs, considering factors like natural hair texture, porosity, and ingredients to get optimal results.     FAQ's

    Is it good to put a texturizer on natural hair?

    Yes, as compared to hair relaxers, texturizers are only applied to the hair for a few minutes to help loosen the texture of your curls. Hence, they aren't quite damaging to the strands.

    How many minutes should texturizer stay in hair?

    Hair texturizers for natural hair should be applied to your hair for a maximum of 10 minutes for even-distributed results. Texturizers, like hair relaxers, work to modify your natural texture to make your curls more manageable.

    Can a texturizer be used on all types of natural hair?

    Texturizers are best suited to S-shaped curls and naturally wavy hair. However, the outcome of Z-shaped curls may differ from person to person. A texturizer is most commonly used on coarse black hair.

    Can a texturizer damage natural hair?

    When applied for more than 10 minutes, the texturizer can damage color-treated hair if containing any harsh chemicals.

    Should I consult a professional stylist before using a texturizer on my natural hair?

    Yes, you can determine what hair type you have and can try a hair texturizer that matches your needs.

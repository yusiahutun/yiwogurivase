This chic needlework technique is perfect for the warmer temperatures

    Broderie anglaise is a traditional technique which is believed to have originated in Czech Republic and was brought to England at the turn of the 19th century.

    The style of fabric has been in fashion in Blighty ever since, with women of the era wholeheartedly embracing the needlework fabric which incorporates features of embroidery, cutwork and needle lace - so much so that now the textile is associated with England.

    It is characterised by holes, called eyelets, which are cut out of the fabric, then bound with overcast or buttonhole stitches and made into patterns on the garment.

    How to style broderie anglaise 

    The chic needlework technique is something of a summer style classic, and it has even received the royal stamp of approval. The Princess of Wales has worn pieces featuring broderie anglaise on multiple public appearances, adding to the technique's reputation as a signal of feminine polish.  

    Broderie anglaise exudes elegance, making it perfect for the races, Wimbledon or any other chic spring/summer occasion. But it's also hugely versatile and can easily be incorporated into a holiday capsule wardrobe in the form of an airy beach coverup or a vibrant mini.

    How we chose:

    • Style: Less of a trend and more of a classic, broderie anglaise has a certain timeless quality. Our selections vary in design but they are united by the fact that they have a certain lasting appeal and can be styled in multiple ways.
    • Price: Variety is key, and therefore alongside pieces by some of our favourite designer labels, we've included some stylish picks from the high street if you're looking to save.
    • Quality: Many of our picks are created from airy, durable cotton. The intricacy of the technique means that some picks are dry clean only, to preserve the quality of the garment.

Las Vegas mansion where Elvis and Michael Jackson stayed on sale for $3.5m

October 09, 2024
blog

On a quiet street off the Strip in Las Vegas sits a vast home that really is one-of-a-kind and has just come onto the market.

The bright white building with red painted accents on Sixth Street near downtown Las Vegas has a rich history to match its uniquely opulent interiors.

The Hartland Mansion was originally two houses in the 1940s, when Sixth Street was a fashionable address and home to the likes of Howard Hughes.

In the early 1970s, the two houses were joined together by larger than life Casino owner Lawrence Arvey, who sent his architect to Disneyland to copy the exterior of the Plaza Inn on Main Street.

The extravagant foyer of the Hartland Mansion, with its two curved staircases and checkerboard floor, was inspired by 'Gone with the Wind' 

Gold accents, flowers, and black and white can be found throughout the house

The outside of the Hartland Mansion was based on the Plaza Inn on Main Street in Disneyland

Inspired by Versailles, but with a Vegas twist, the home has numerous chandeliers and luxurious fabrics throughout

Celebrities that are said to have visited the original house from the 1940s to the late 1970s include icons such as Elvis Presley, Frank Sinatra, Dean Martin, Tony Bennett, Sammy Davis Jr, Liberace, Zsa Zsa and Eva Gabor, Mae West, Esther Williams, Marilyn Monroe, Marlene Dietriech, Groucho Marx, Rosemary Clooney and Ronald Reagan.

The mansion gained its name from the next owner, Toni Hart, a former evangelist and musician whose family recorded more than 20 gospel albums.

She bought the property for $190,000 in 1978 after Arvey fled the country while out on bond and facing life in prison for sex crimes The New York Times reported in 2010. He has never been caught.

The bedrooms are particularly dramatically decorated and have seen their share of famous guests, including Elvis Presley

Toni Harts bedroom features a white balustrade around the bed and a 34 pound pearl-encrusted bedspread

The house has eight bedrooms, nine full bathrooms and four half bathrooms

Not long after she had purchased the house there were two fires, one in 1980 and one in 1981. 

Hart decided to rebuild using the original design, but more than doubling the size of the property from 13,000 square-feet to an enormous 31,000 square-feet.

The project took seven years to complete and, with the help of her son, Larry, Hart was able to stamp her own unique taste on the interiors.

The entrance hall features a dramatic 'Gone with the Wind'-inspired curved double staircase, black and white checkerboard floor and a 35 foot-high dome.

Michael Jackson and Elvis are among the many visitors to the Hartland Mansion over the years

Robert DeNiro and Sharon Stone shots scenes for the 1995 movie 'Casino' in the living room of the mansion

Rococo plasterwork, chandeliers, extravagant drapes, cherubs and rich fabrics adorn every room and are inspired by the Palace of Versailles.

Hartland is by far the largest house on the block, and the living room alone measures 33 feet by 57 feet – large enough to accommodate a production crew to shoot part of the 1995 movie 'Casino' starring Robert DeNiro and Sharon Stone. 

The property features eight bedrooms, nine full bathrooms and four half-baths. One bedroom commemorates Elvis Presley once having stayed in the original house as a guest of Arvey's. It includes a cardboard cutout of the King of Rock 'n' Roll.

This dining room features a lavish tented ceiling

Thick blue carpet and gilded furniture fill the living room

Every surface is covered with object d'art and sculptures 

The music room features a mirrored piano and a display of photographs of celebrities that have visited since the Harts moved in.

Two different permits allowed the house to be both Hart's home and a commercial property used for weddings and other events.

According to the website for the event space: 'Ginger Rogers danced here. Michael Jackson moonwalked here. Willie Nelson sang here. Engelbert Humperdinck dined here. Cee Lo Green, Miss Piggy and the Muppets filmed a music video here. Robert De Niro and Sharon Stone made a movie here. Jackie Collins launched a book here.'

The Grand Party Room is centered on a huge indoor pool with a striking black, white and floral design 

The room can accommodate 400 people and is used for weddings, fashion shows, concerts and other events

The Las Vegas Symphony once gave a concert for 400 guests in the Grand Party Room, which features an indoor swimming pool. 

The event space has also been used for art and fashion shows, fundraisers, corporate events, Bar and Bat Mitzvahs, weddings and receptions.

'The glamour of old Las Vegas is alive and well at Hartland Mansion,' says the website.

More recent guests include Debbie Reynolds, Ozzie Osbourne, Willie Nelson, Frankie Valli and a host of politicians. 

Toni Hart passed away in 2014 and the whole compound is now for sale for $3.5 million. 

Outside is a second pool and a charming gazebo

Red accents are found all the way around the entire bright white exterior of the building

Statues of angels and cherubs can be found throughout the house and garden

The whole property is for sale for $3.5 million following the death of owner Toni Hart

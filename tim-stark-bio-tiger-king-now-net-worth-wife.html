Where Is Tiger King’s Tim Stark Now?

Tim Stark from Tiger King faced several legal issues that even got him to court. He was taken into custody in April 2021 despite not facing any criminal charges. He was in a long fight to keep Wildlife in Need up and running and to keep the exotic animals that he owned.

Scott Maples, chief deputy of the Clark County Sheriff’s Department, didn’t make clear elaboration of why he was arrested. But, he stated that Tim was taken into custody for “non-criminal-related concerns.” In 2020, the United States Department of Agriculture (USDA) wrote a 183-page report.

The report stated that Tim, over the years, had harassed federal inspectors. He also failed to provide veterinary care to some sick or dying animals. Furthermore, he even beat a young leopard to death with a baseball bat. In September 2020, a judge ruled that the Indianapolis Zoological Society will be taking control of all the animals at Wildlife in Need.

Later, the Indianapolis Zoo was ordered to keep all the animals rescued from Wildlife in Need.

Tim went on an Instagram live and argued, “I’m demanding that we do a virtual, public hearing,” Stark said. “I want to reason behind your ruling. Now, I know judge, you’re probably thinking, ‘Who in the f*** is Tim Stark to demand anything from the almighty judge? Well, let me introduce you to somebody … I want to introduce you to PETA.”

The judge who ruled the judgment also shared that when Stark held up his arm, it showed what appeared to be a syringe wrapped around his wrist. While in custody, investigators said that he grabbed and threatened an Indiana deputy attorney general in March during a zoo inspection.

In June 2021, Tim again made the headlines after the court ordered him to pay more than $700k.

NYPD officer Nancy Heinz could lose her job for dating a convicted murder

March 22, 2024
blog

A police officer known as the Marilyn Monroe of the NYPD could possibly face internal charges for dating a convicted killer.

Staten Island officer Nancy Heinz, 53, reportedly had a rocky relationship with 48-year-old ex-con Mark Liverano. 

Their romance only came to light after the parolee was busted during a fight with another man inside Hot Shotz, a sports bar in Staten Island, last Friday night, the New York Daily News reported.

The blonde beauty was reportedly inside of the bar during the fight that involved Liverano, who served 19 years in prison for killing a father-of-two. 

Mark Liverano (left) reportedly had a rocky relationship with Staten Island officer Nancy Heinz (right)

Liverano (above) served 19 years in prison for killing a father-of-two. He was convicted of manslaughter and paroled in August 2014

He pleaded guilty to manslaughter in 1995 and was paroled in August 2014.

In Friday's incident, Liverano allegedly attacked Michael Davis, a retired police officer who is a friend of Heinz. 

During the attack, Liverano allegedly struck Davis with a 'black pistol-shaped object' that he pulled out from his waistband, the Daily News reported.

Davis needed 17 staples to close the deep head gash he suffered during the attack, according to court records. 

Liverano is also accused of flashing what appeared to be a gun at Heinz Friday. 

According to the Daily News, Liverano is facing assault charges in the attack against Davis and apparently violated an order of protection that Heinz filed against him after a previous incident. 

The latest altercation reportedly involving Liverano happened last Friday at Hot Shotz (above) in Staten Island

'I don't want you in here,' Liverano screamed at Heinz according to the court records. 'F-----g leave the bar!' 

In court documents obtained by the Daily News, Heinz, who joined the police force in 1989, described herself as his ex-girlfriend. 

Nancy Heinz works at the 120th Precinct in Staten Island and became a police officer in 1989

The ex-con's family confirmed that the pair had dated from January to July of this year and that they appeared to be a happy, the Daily News reported. 

His sister, Marie Liverano, said that after they broke up, Heinz would 'taunt him and goad him into violence,' the paper reported. 

'She kept seeking him out,' Marie Liverano told the News. 'She's just no good. 

'He was doing everything right until he met her.

'He's just trying to put his life back together.' 

Law enforcement sources also told the Daily News that Liverano spit on Heinz during a separate confrontation at another restaurant in September. 

Liverano pleaded guilty to manslaughter in 1995 after 37-year-old bouncer Richard Boeke was shot while trying to break up a fight at a restaurant in Brooklyn, New York. 

According to the NYPD Patrol Guide, Police officers caught fraternizing with a known felon can be hit with a violation and possibly lose their jobs or face loss of vacation days.

It's unclear if Heinz, who works at the 120th Precinct in Staten Island, knew that she was dating a convicted killer. 

The blonde's now ex-husband, Police Officer Mark Faljean, was convicted in 2004 of molesting a colleague's wife and sentenced to sex offender treatment, the Daily News reported.

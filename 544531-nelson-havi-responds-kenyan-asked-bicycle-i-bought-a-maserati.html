Former Law Society of Kenya (LSK) president Nelson is known for taking no prisoners when dealing with his critics on social media.

Nelson Havi's political career

Havi, a vocal lawyer who unsuccessfully vied for the Westlands seat in the National Assembly, is among the country's most celebrated lawyers who have tried a hand in politics.

His popularity soared when he and his colleagues successfully opposed the Building Bridges Initiative plot to amend the Constitution of Kenya 2010.

Since declaring his political interest, Havi dumped Musalia Mudavadi's Amani National Congress (ANC) party and joined President William Ruto's United Democratic Alliance (UDA), through which he contested for the Westlands seat.

This explains his inclination towards the current regime in political discussions.

Nelson Havi criticises Gabriel Oguda's fluency

Giving his analysis of Azimio la Umoja One Kenya Coalition diehard Gabriel Oguda's interview with former NTV news anchor Mark Masai, Havi poured cold water on his fluency and articulation of issues.

"I expected Gabriel Oguda to speak eloquently, as the articles attributed to him suggest. He falls short of adding ‘nini’, ‘ginene’, and ‘ng’ane’ to his sentences," Havi posted on X.

Oguda is known for criticising the government and Havi and even nicknamed the lawyer Chai Guevara after the former LSK president's move to have tea with MPs.

This was after Chief Justice Emeritus David Maraga said the Parliament of Kenya was unconstitutionally constituted regarding gender.

Nelson Havi's Bicycle Riding Culture

Havi's criticism of Oguda brought about a political confrontation between supporters of the two parties.

This saw one Kenyan on X ask Havi where he had taken his bicycle and riding culture after losing the 2022 elections to Tim Wanyonyi.

The Kenyan insinuated that Havi was using the bicycle as a campaign tool to appeal to low-income Kenyans, but the campaign failed terribly.

In 2021, Havi embraced a bicycle-riding culture as a way to cut costs by reducing fuel expenditures, saying it was not only cost-effective but also healthy.

"During campaigns, we used to see you riding a bicycle around the city. What happened, my brother?" Hezron Wafula posed.

Who makes Maserati cars?

In a quick rejoinder to the question about his bicycle and riding culture, Havi sarcastically hinted that he had upgraded his means of transport.

"I bought a Maserati," said Havi in reference to an elegant Italian sportscar that was a preserve of the wealthy.

Another Kenyan flowed with Havi's sarcasm and told him to seek votes from the sports car he had bought during the next elections.

"Ambia hiyo Maserati ikupigie kura (tell that Maserati to vote for you)," Charles Kiptoo said.

Despite being on Kenya Kwanza, Havi congratulated NARC Kenya leader Martha Karua for being selected as the running mate of Azimio la Umoja One Kenya Coalition presidential flagbearer Raila Odinga.

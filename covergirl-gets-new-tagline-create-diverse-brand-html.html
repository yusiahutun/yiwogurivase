CoverGirl gets new tagline to create a more diverse brand

April 28, 2024
blog

No one knows how to do a makeover better than CoverGirl - and now the beauty brand has turned that transformative power inwards in order to re-invent its public image, starting with the loss of its iconic 60-year-old slogan. 

And to take it a step further, the company is not only eschewing its 'Easy, Breezy, Beautiful' tagline in favor of something more 'bold', it is is also changing its logo, tone, packaging and product design.  

The brand explained that motive behind the redesign was to create a line that will 'inspire people to embrace their unique identities and unapologetically create any version of themselves through make-up'.

Making a change: Spokesmodels like Katy Perry (left) and 69-year-old model Maye Musk (right) are taking part in the re-branding efforts, which have been celebrated with a new video ad

A thing of the past: The previous tagline was used across CoverGirl branding and ads for 60 years, including many of its celebrity campaigns, including this Zendaya shoot

'The beauty category and consumer have changed dramatically since we launched 'Easy, Breezy, Beautiful' nearly 20 years ago,' Ukonwa Ojo, SVP at CoverGirl, said in a statement.

'People don't think of beauty as one-dimensional, or matching traditional ideals and standards of perfection. Beauty should be diverse, dynamic and authentic.'

CoverGirl and its ad agency, Droga5, discovered that people no longer think of make-up just as a cosmetic but rather as an instrument used for self-expression and the company hopes to show that in their redesign.  

'From a woman rocking a bold purple lip in the board room, to a teen wearing winged eyeliner while manning the cash register, makeup today is bold and colorful - very different from easy and breezy,' Katy Alonzo, group strategy director at Droga5, said of the move.

'The core challenge for us was to modernize CoverGirl for the modern woman.'

Beauty is diversity: Issa Rae, star of the HBO series Insecure, was also added to the line-up of spokesmodels being featured in CoverGirl's newly re-branded campaign video

But it appears that the agency was successful in the creation of their new tagline, which was designed to question people' perceptions around make-up and where and why it's worn. 

The company wants its customers to believe they have the ability to do what they want, when they want with make-up in the hopes if making the brand stand out against other beauty lines such as Sephora. 

Shannon Washington, the creative director at Droga5 said that CoverGirl couldn't stick to the norm 'of how we communicated our message out' because today's women want to see themselves portrayed in beauty brands.  

And to kick-off the redesign centered on diversity and 'unique identities' CoverGirl launched an anthem video 'Made in the Mirror' that was released yesterday. 

CoverGirl diversified their spokesmodel line-up when they added Zendaya, Sofia Vergara, Janelle Monae and male beauty vlogger James Charles.

Fit and fierce: Fitness trainer Massy Arias also stars in the new campaign, which aims to embrace the more 'bold' approach many women have to make-up 

Motorcycles and make-up: Shelina Moreda, a pro motorcycle racer, makes a powerful statement in her campaign shot from the brand's 'Made in the Mirror' video

Welcome to the family: Long-time brand ambassador Katy shared her excitement at having new faces on boar, as well as her joy at the new slogan

Unimaginable: Issa also shared on Twitter her excitement after being named a CoverGirl

But that wasn't enough for the beauty brand who also decided to include actress Issa Rae, TV personality Ayesha Curry, 69-year-old model Maye Musk, fitness trainer Massy Arias, professional motorcycle racer Shelina Moreda and longtime CoverGirl Katy Perry who are all featured in the anthem video.

Some of them even took to Twitter to share their excitement about being part of CoverGirl's redesign.  

All of these women have combined their professions with their love for make-up, while empowering and encouraging others to do the same - no matter the occupation, appearance or age.  

'CoverGirl has always been inclusive and is known for pushing the boundaries of what it means to be beautiful, which means we have a responsibility to elevate how we connect and communicate with people,' Ukonwa Ojo said. 

'This is bigger than a new campaign or a tagline. We hope to spark a provocative dialogue that shifts cultural assumptions about when, where, how and why people wear make-up.'

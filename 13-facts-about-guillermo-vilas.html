Vilas dominated the clay courts.

Throughout his career, Vilas proved to be virtually unbeatable on clay courts, earning him the nickname “The King of Clay.” With a record-breaking career total of 659 wins on clay, including an incredible 53 consecutive victories, Vilas has cemented his place as one of the greatest clay court players of all time.

He won four Grand Slam titles.

Vilas claimed victory in four Grand Slam tournaments during his illustrious career, including the French Open in 1977, the Australian Open in 1978, and the US Open in 1977 and His exceptional achievements on the court solidify his status as one of Argentina’s most successful tennis players.

Vilas held the longest winning streak in tennis history.

In 1977, Vilas achieved a remarkable feat by winning 46 consecutive matches, breaking the previous record of 36 set by Björn Borg. This incredible winning streak further highlights Vilas’ dominance and skill on the tennis court.

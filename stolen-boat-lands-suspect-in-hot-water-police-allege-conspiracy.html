UPDATE: Stolen boat lands suspects in hot water: Police allege conspiracy

July 21, 2024
blog

Facebook Messenger, surveillance video were part of investigation

Two Davenport men face felony charges after police allege they had a stolen fishing boat after conspiring via Facebook Messenger.

Chad Finn, 48, faces charges of first-degree theft and conspiracy to commit a non-forcible felony, court records say.

Nickolas White Sr., 45, faces charges of first-degree theft, third-degree burglary and conspiracy to commit a non-forcible felony, according to court records.

On Oct. 5, 2022, Davenport Police responded to the 8600 block of Northwest Boulevard in reference to a reported burglary, arrest affidavits say.

According to affidavits, police allege White burglarized a storage unit to gain access to a boat – a 2014 LUND 1875 Crossover XS – which was locked within the unit.

Finn, police allege, communicated via Facebook Messenger with White regarding the boat. On Oct. 2, 2022, White asked Finn if he would like a new fishing boat, and Finn replied yes on the same day, according to affidavits.

On Oct. 3, at about 12:09 a.m., White messaged Finn: “I’m going to get your boat.” About 3:11 a.m. a truck entered the access road to a unit in the 8600 block of Northwest Boulevard and left with the victim’s boat about 3:18 a.m., police allege in affidavits.

A message at about 3:35 a.m. from White says “Your fishing boat is in front of your house.” The same truck that had taken the boat from the storage unit dropped it off at an address on West 46th Street, Davenport, police allege in affidavits.

Finn was captured on security video taking physical possession of the boat by moving it into his driveway with his vehicle. He searched the boat and removed items from it, then transported the boat to the 3800 block of Wapello Avenue, according to affidavits.

Finn allegedly sold some of the items to a third party. These later were discovered by law enforcement and seized, affidavits say.

Finn worked in conspiracy with White “to promote and facilitate the commission of a crime which, due to the dollar amount and action, was a felony,” police allege in affidavits.

According to affidavits, the estimated total value of the stolen boat, trailer and goods is about $70,000.

Finn was released on a $10,000 cash-only bond to appear Feb. 1 at a preliminary hearing in Scott County Court.

White is being held on a total $25,000 bond in Scott County Jail. He waived a preliminary hearing, and is set for arraignment on Jan. 26 in Scott County Court.

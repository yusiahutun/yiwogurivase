John Henderson wins World Seniors Darts Championship with fans absolutely delighted

September 16, 2024
blog

John Henderson has become the World Seniors Darts champion at the first time of asking.

Henderson, competing in the event for the first time, defeated Colin McGarry 5-0 in the final. The 50-year-old Scotsman never looked like losing in the one-sided match up which saw him collect the coveted trophy and a cheque for £30,000.

'Big John', as he was known by fans during his days in the PDC, is a former Tour Card holder and major winner, having triumphed in the World Cup of Darts with Peter Wright in 2021. He has also been a challenger in the Premier League and reached the semi-finals of the World Grand Prix as recently as 2017.

Henderson lost his Tour Card in 2022 and has failed to regain it since, coming up short in the most recent edition of Q School. That, combined with the fact he is now 50, allowed him to play in the Seniors World Championship, which is held at the Circus Tavern.

And the Scotsman showed his class throughout the tournament as he defeated all before him to claim the title. Henderson came through tests against Trina Gulliver, Kevin Painter, Andy Hamilton and Jim Long – averaging 92.91 against the latter – to book his spot in the final.

He routed McGarry 5-0 in the final itself, dropping just six legs en route to victory. Henderson also averaged 86.72 during the match, which saw him become seniors champion for the first time, much to the delight of darts fans.

One wrote: "Outstanding performance in the final there from big Hendo. Fully deserved." While another said: "Couldn’t of happened to a nicer bloke." As a third added: "Well played John Henderson. Played really well in the final. Good run from Colin McGarry."

Speaking afterwards, Henderson said: "Winning the World Cup with Peter Wright was great but this is right up there. It’s good to keep the trophy north of the border after two years with Robert. It’s massive for me."

However, it was a tournament to forget for 16-time world champion Phil Taylor, who crashed out in the first round to little-known German star Manfred Bilderl. The Power averaged just 74.53 in defeat in what could prove to be his last game on TV.

Jessica Rabbit is a legendary cartoon character created by Gary K. Wolf.

Jessica Rabbit is known for her voluptuous figure, sultry voice, and stunning red hair. She was brought to life in the iconic film “Who Framed Roger Rabbit” directed by Robert Zemeckis.

Jessica Rabbit is not actually a rabbit.

Despite her last name, Jessica Rabbit is not a rabbit. She is a stunningly beautiful and curvaceous human who happens to be married to the titular character, Roger Rabbit.

Jessica Rabbit’s signature line is “I’m not bad, I’m just drawn that way.”

This iconic line perfectly captures Jessica Rabbit’s seductive and mysterious nature. It has become one of the most memorable quotes in cartoon history.

Jessica Rabbit’s character design was inspired by several iconic actresses.

When creating Jessica Rabbit’s character design, the animators drew inspiration from legendary actresses such as Lauren Bacall, Veronica Lake, and Rita Hayworth. Her hourglass figure and glamorous style pay homage to these Hollywood icons.

Jessica Rabbit was voiced by Kathleen Turner.

Kathleen Turner provided the sultry and alluring voice for Jessica Rabbit in the film. Her deep and captivating voice perfectly matched the character’s seductive persona.

Jessica Rabbit’s dress was custom-made for her character.

The infamous red sequined dress that Jessica Rabbit wears was custom-made for her character. It was designed to enhance her curves and exude glamour and allure.

Jessica Rabbit’s character became a sex symbol.

Due to her stunning beauty and sensual allure, Jessica Rabbit quickly became a symbol of sexiness in the world of cartoons. She is often regarded as one of the sexiest animated characters ever created.

Jessica Rabbit has a strong fan following.

Over the years, Jessica Rabbit has gathered a dedicated fan base who admire both her physical beauty and her intriguing persona. She continues to be a popular character at conventions and cosplay events.

Jessica Rabbit’s famous quote was ad-libbed.

The famous line “I’m not bad, I’m just drawn that way” was actually ad-libbed by Kathleen Turner during the recording session. It has since become one of the most memorable lines in film history.

Jessica Rabbit’s animated performance in “Who Framed Roger Rabbit” was groundbreaking at the time of its release. The seamless integration of live-action and animation created a revolutionary viewing experience.

Jessica Rabbit’s character has inspired various merchandise.

As a beloved character, Jessica Rabbit has inspired a wide range of merchandise, including dolls, clothing, and collectibles. Fans can express their adoration for her character through these various items.

Jessica Rabbit has appeared in other media.

Aside from her iconic role in “Who Framed Roger Rabbit,” Jessica Rabbit has made appearances in video games, comic books, and theme park attractions. Her character has continued to captivate audiences across different platforms.

Jessica Rabbit’s iconic red hair is a wig.

Jessica Rabbit’s character stands as a symbol of mystery.

Throughout the film, Jessica Rabbit remains an enigmatic figure, leaving audiences intrigued and curious about her true nature. Her air of mystery adds to her allure and makes her an unforgettable character.

Jessica Rabbit’s character has stood the test of time.

Decades after her debut, Jessica Rabbit remains an iconic and recognizable character. Her timeless appeal ensures that she will continue to be celebrated in popular culture for years to come.

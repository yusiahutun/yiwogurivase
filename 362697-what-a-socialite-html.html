What exactly is a socialite?

The origin of the word socialite can be traced back to the 1920s when it was associated with being socially recognisable and wealthy. According to the Cambridge dictionary, a socialite is someone from a high-class society famous for attending numerous parties and social events.

The title was often given to royal wives, mistresses, or lovers both male and female. That is why when you look up questions such as "what is another word for socialite?" you are likely to get a wide range of meanings like aristocrat, blue-blooded, swell, noble, gentleperson, magnate and mogul.

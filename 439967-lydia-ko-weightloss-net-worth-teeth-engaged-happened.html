Career

Lydia started playing golf at five years old after her mom took her to the Pupuke Golf Club on Auckland's North Shore. She met her first coach, Guy Wilson, who trained her until 2013 when she started working with David Leadbetter.

The pro golfer was propelled to the limelight at seven years old when she competed in the New Zealand national amateur championships. In 2012, when she was 14 years old, Ko became the youngest person to win a professional golf tour event when she bagged the Lee/Samsung Women's NSW Open on the ALPG Tour.

Later that year, the female golfer claimed another title of the youngest winner of an LPGA (Ladies Professional Golf Association) Tour event at the Canadian Women's Championships.

She has claimed several other victories, including the Swinging Skirts LPGA Classic, her first LPGA Tour win as a professional. This was her first win on American soil, and she has gained popularity over other players like American pro golfer, Lexi Thompson.

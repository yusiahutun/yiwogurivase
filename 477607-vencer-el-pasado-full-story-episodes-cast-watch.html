However, they realize that whatever is posted on social media can never be deleted. For this reason, they must devise a solution for living in the present. Vencer el Pasado full story

Vencer el Pasado follows the story of Renata Sanchez Vidal. She is a young and famous molecular biologist. She has a bright future and is also about to marry the love of her life, Alonso Cancino. But, unfortunately, things start going south after facing accusations of infidelity. In a video, she appears passionately kissing a stranger, hence the traitor bride allegations.

The clip goes viral, leading to several negative implications, such as losing her job and the cancellation of her wedding. Shortly, she discovers that her fiancée has copyrighted her valuable research project. She struggles a lot but eventually manages to secure a job with a high-end genetic laboratory, Biogenelab.

Despite working in a sexist environment, she receives great support from the company's owner, Lisandro Mascaro. As a result, Alonso also secures a job in the same company. Shortly after, the company hires Mauro Alvarez, a computer scientist to whom Renata starts getting attracted. Little does she know that Mauro is only after avenging his father.

At first, Mauro and Renata cannot get along, with Mauro questioning her professional capacity. Later, Carmen Medina joins the company as their new receptionist. She left her hometown after seeing explicit videos of her husband’s multiple infidelities, who was also abusive. Carmen and her children, Ulises, Oliver and Danna, live in Mexico.

After seeing her family fall apart, she becomes addicted to social media and her mobile phone. She creates a life that will eventually get her closer to a dangerous predator. Another woman in the company is Mariluz Blanco. Blanco fled a digital lynching after her boyfriend leaked their sex tape. Unfortunately, her past comes to light just when she seems to have overcome the past.

She receives an offer to be a surrogate in a bid to secure her future financially. Renata finds out that she suffers from chronic liver disease. The illness makes her realize that she is not part of the family she grew up in. Therefore, the four women, Renata, Mariluz, Carmen and Danna, share a strong bond that unites them while struggling against all the social inhumanities they have experienced in their past.

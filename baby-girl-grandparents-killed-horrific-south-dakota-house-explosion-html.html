Baby girl and grandparents killed in horrific South Dakota house explosion

May 30, 2024
blog

A massive house explosion in South Dakota has killed a six-month old baby girl and her two grandparents - with two children, 5 and 7, also in critical condition. 

Ladonna Hupp, 61, and six-month-old Harper Hupp were found dead at the scene. William "Bill" Hupp, 66, was transported to Avera St. Mary's Hospital in Pierre, South Dakota, where he succumbed to his injuries.

Myles, five, and Royce, three, were airlifted to a burn unit in Minnesota, where they remain in critical condition. Myles suffered burns over 10 percent of his body and Royce was burned over 25 percent of his body, according to a Caring Bridge Site set up for the family. 

The children's parents Trevor and Kelsey Hupp were at work when the explosion happened. 

The blast, which occurred Wednesday, sent debris flying 50 to 100 yards in all directions and completely leveled their home, along Highway 1806 in the Antelope Creek area in Stanley County. The only structure still standing was some playground equipment.

A GoFundMe has been created for the family and has already raised $30,000

The house about 11 miles southwest of Fort Pierre exploded around 10:20 a.m. Wednesday, Stanley County Sheriff Brad Rathbun said. 

The cause of the explosion is unknown, but at this point foul play is not suspected, Rathbun said. 

A neighbor initially reported hearing a loud explosion Wednesday morning and alerted authorities. 

A GoFundMe has been created for the family by coworker and friend Cindy Erwin, who describes the Hupps as an amazing family.

It reads: 'Hello everyone, we are raising funds for an amazing family in our local community of Pierre, SD. Kelsey is nurse practitioner that I have had the privilege to work with, and Trevor is her husband.

'Today, they had a tragic accident at the home of their dreams. While Kelsey and Trevor were at work, a major explosion occurred resulting not only in the total loss of their home, but the loss of their infant daughter, Harper, and both of Trevor's parents as well.'

The GoFundMe has already raised around $30,000 after setting an initial goal of $10,000.

The South Dakota State Fire Marshal’s Office says the cause of the explosion is being investigated.

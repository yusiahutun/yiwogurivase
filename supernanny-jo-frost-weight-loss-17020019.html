Supernanny Jo Frost reveals the truth behind her epic body transformation

April 07, 2024
blog

The Supernanny star returned to the small screen for a parenting segment on This Morning, but it was her svelte physique that ended up stealing the show.

Now the 45-year-old has opened up about how she slimmed down around her hectic telly schedule.

Rather than adopt a drastic diet or hit the gym relentlessly, Jo simply made a series of small, sustainable changes to her relationship with food.

Celeb weight loss transformations View gallery

She stressed that she still had "cheat days" where she ate what she liked and would never resort to going hungry to shift the pounds.

"I am just trying to live as well as I can within the hours of my work. I’m still trying to work out a balance and I have my off days like everyone else," she explained.

"It starts with being more mindful about what choices you are making with your food. I don’t beat myself up about it too much if I’m not able to follow it, though."

She stressed that the odd spot of indulgence was actually a great way to develop willpower and avoid the pitfalls of an "all or nothing" approach.

"I recognise that every time you fall off [the diet] and you can get yourself back on it, it actually does make you mentally stronger – not all is lost," she added to The Sun.

"You have to remember that tomorrow is a new day and you can be more conscious of the choice you made before and try not to repeat."

Following her rise to fame back in 2014, Jo jetted off to America and Europe to launch international versions of Supernanny.

While she tends to remain quiet about her personal life, she revealed on Loose Women last year that she was swamped with wedding planning.

She also opened up about whether she would consider having her own children.

"I have to say for families that foster, for families that adopt, for the professional nannies out there that are as integral and dedicated as myself in my profession with what I was doing, you don’t biologically need to have a child to feel love and to connect with children," she said.

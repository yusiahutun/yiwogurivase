Eleanor Tomlinson beauty interview

February 23, 2024
blog

Since finding fame in much-loved period drama Poldark, British actress and singer Eleanor Tomlinson has become a red carpet fixture, with her graceful, feminine glamour topping international best-dressed lists. Tomlinson's beauty look, too, is the fodder of Pinterest boards; her rich red hair (a transformation she took on to portray Demelza Poldark, and kept) stands out in a sea of balayage-blondes, enhancing her 'English rose' elegance. Below, she shares with Bazaar her beauty philosophy.

How I prepare for a red carpet look

"I work with a wonderful team which usually consists of Rebecca Corbin-Murray, stylist extraordinaire; Justine Jenkins for cruelty-free make-up; Perrine Rougemont, hairstylist and all-round genius; and Victoria Raeburn-Wales, the best publicist in town and a great friend. Before every event, we all discuss each look and bring different ideas to the table. Leaving the house for an event is a collaboration and these very inspiring women fill me with the confidence I need to be able to go out there and strut my stuff. Every look is different, and I’m not afraid to try new things – it’s how we learn."

In my make-up bag you’ll find...

"My favourite make-up products include the Giorgio Armani Luminous Silk Foundation for dewy coverage, and their Neo Nude A-Blush as it is multi-tasking for lips and cheeks – it feels so light on the skin and is easy to blend. The Hourglass Brow Micro Sculpting Pencil for precision brow coverage and Soap Brows for shaping with a MyKitCo thick brush. Kat Von D Anti-Precision Pencil for a no budge eye liner and Code Beautiful mascara. For lips, any shade in the Rouge d'Armani Sheer collection; Hourglass also do some stunning lips collections, and Burt’s Bees have a great collection of lip balms that are both colourful and moisturising."

Less is more: I prefer minimal make-up

"I try not to wear that much make-up. I think it’s a shame when you see beautiful young girls caked in make-up. Embrace your natural beauty, embrace your skin. Less is more. Use make-up to give you confidence but don’t use it as a mask to hide behind. You are enough."

My beauty philosophy is…

"Look after your skin so you don’t need that much make-up – and be proud of what you were born with. Everyone is unique, beauty is the confidence you have in yourself."

I use a moisturiser beneath and on top of make-up

"After cleansing with the Organic Pharmacy Carrot Butter Cleanser I use the XYZ Smart Collagen moisturiser with the Inlight Beauty Under Eye Revive Balm. This is also gorgeous for adding moisture back into skin over a foundation base. Using moisturiser to add in that dewy effect is one of the best tips I’ve learnt from make-up artists."

I’m actually a natural blonde

"I dyed my hair red for Poldark, but I’ll definitely be keeping it red. I love it! As a redhead compared to blonde, I feel more confident. I feel strong. I feel more me."

Colouring hair is such a science

"Katie Hale at Charles Worthington in London does my colour. Colouring hair is such a science – finding the right tone for your skin, achieving that vibrancy and the exact colour you want. She’s a genius. If you’re thinking about a change-up of your colour, she’s the one you want to see. To keep the vibrancy, Kérastase do a great colour boost you add in to your conditioner. Reds fade so fast and this really helps maintain my colour in-between my appointments."

I believe in inside-out beauty

"I try and eat well and exercise, and get plenty of fresh air. It really helps my skin."

My personal trainer is my mentor

"I have a personal trainer who is my mentor. I hate gyms, but he makes working out fun and then we go for a bacon sandwich as my treat if I do well!"

When it comes to self-care, it’s important to limit social media

"I try and create a space where I feel relaxed. I have a Neom diffuser that I put essential oils into which helps me to feel calm and enjoy the space around me. I also surround myself with positive people and stay off social media as much as possible. It can be a positive platform but it’s important not to live your life on it."

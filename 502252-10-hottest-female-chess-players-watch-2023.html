So, who are the hottest female chess players?

Numerous impressive female chess grandmasters have done a commendable job at competitions, proving their worth to the world. But who among the best ranks as the most beautiful?

1. Regina Pokorna

Regina Pokorna is a Slovak-Austrian chess player born on January 18, 1982. She holds the title of Woman Grandmaster (WGM). The player is not only famous for her mastery of chess, which has seen her win numerous tournaments, but her incredible beauty.

Grandmaster Ján Plachetka trained Regina, and indeed, she became the best. In 1992, Regina won the European girls under ten championship and the Slovak girls under 18 titles in 1996, 1997, and 1999. Her other notable wins include the European Girls’ Chess Championship in Patras, Greece. In 2019, Regina won the Austrian Women’s Chess Championship.

2. Anna Sharevich

Anna Sharevich ranks as one of the best among the world-renowned, beautiful chess players. Interestingly, the chess player still holds the FIDE of Woman Grandmaster title today. She studied at the Saratov State Academy of Law and showed immense dedication to chess.

The Belarusian-American Woman Grandmaster was born on December 18, 1985, and is in her late thirties. She won the Belarusian Chess Championships in 2002, 2005, 2007, and 2011.

3. Alexandra Botez

Alexandra Botex is one of the hottest female chess players. You have likely watched her numerous times on her YouTube channel, Botezlive, which she runs with her sister Andrea. The YouTube channel is dedicated to chess. She also streams games on Twitch, where she plays and commentates.

Alexandra is an American-Canadian chess player, born in 1995, which makes her one of the youngest female chess Grandmasters. She was born to Romanian immigrant parents and raised in Vancouver, British Columbia, Canada.

4. Anna Muzychuk

Anna Muzychuk is a Ukrainian female player. Something interesting about her is that she was among the only four female chess players to attend a minimum of 2600 FDE rating in July 2012.

Muzychuk grew up in a chess family where her younger sister became a Grandmaster. With her parents' help, Anna learned to play chess at age two. And it took her only a short time to start winning tournaments. At age four, she had already won second place in a chess competition for girls.

Anna Muzychuk won the Women’s World Rapid Chess Championship in 2014 and even achieved the Women’s World Blitz Chess Championship twice in 2014 and 2016. The talented chess player represented Slovenia from 2004 to 2014 because of conflicts with the Ukrainian Chess Federation.

5. Anna Rudolf

Anna Rudolf is a professional Hungarian chess player. She is active on YouTube and Twitch and holds the titles of International Master (IM) and Woman Grandmaster. Her exploits in chess have seen her win, three times, the Hungarian women’s national championships and represent Hungary at the Chess Olympiad and the European Team Chess Championships.

6. Dorsa Derakhshani

Dorsa is a female chess player of Iranian origins but representing the United States of America. She started playing for the US after Iran banned her from playing chess for the Iranian national team or any tournaments after participating in the 2017 Gibraltar Chess Festival without wearing a hijab.

Many consider Dorsa a “sex symbol” for chess, considering her killer looks and immense desire to win every tournament. She won the Asian Youth chess championships and three gold medals in 2013 and 2014.

7. Tatiana Kosintseva

Many women in chess are yet to reach the level of Tatiana Kosintseva. She is an immensely talented Russian chess player awarded the title of Grandmaster by FIDE in 2007. The skilled player is a two-time European and three-time Russian women’s champion.

8. The Polgar Sisters (Susan, Sofia and Judith)

The three Hungarian sisters, Susan, Sofia and Judith, are indeed chess Grandmasters. Interestingly, they were part of an educational experiment by their father, Lazlo Polgar. Lazlo tried to prove that her three daughters could make impressive achievements if trained in a specialist subject from a young age.

Today, the three beautiful sisters are making it big in chess. Susan Polgar is the Women’s World Chess Champion from 1996 to 1999. Sofia has represented Hungary in four Chess Olympiads, winning two gold medals, one silver, and one bronze. The youngest, Judith, has achieved the rank of Grandmaster as well.

9. Stavroula Tsolakidou

Stavroula Tsolakidou is a Greek chess player holding the title of International Master and Woman Grandmaster. She earned her Woman FIDE Master title in 2013 after winning the World U14 Girls’ Chess Championship.

10. Alexandra Kosteniuk

Alexandra Kosteniuk is a beautiful chess player from Russia. She won the Women’s World Chess Championships from 2008 to 2010. She is also the former Women’s Rapid Chess Champion (2021).

Alexandra started playing the game of chess at the age of five after being taught by her father. And since then, she has won numerous awards, including one of her first in 1994 – the European Youth Chess Championship (for girls under 10).

Patrick Dempsey’s Age, Early Life, and Background Details

Patrick Galen Dempsey was born on January 13, 1966, in Lewiston, Maine. His father William Allen Dempsey worked as an insurance broker and his mother Amanda (née Casson) worked as a school secretary. Dempsey has European roots as his father is of German, English, Scottish, and distant Swedish descent. He grew up in Buckfield, Maine with 2 older siblings.

Patrick Dempsey attended St. Dominic Regional High School and later moved to Buckfield High School. However, he was not able to graduate from high school. As an adolescent Dempsey was very good at juggling and he participated in several juggling competitions. In 1981 he participated in the International Jugglers’ Association Championships, where he was the first runner-up to the legendary juggler Anthony Gatto.

Just like juggling, acting was a matter of course for the young Patrick Dempsey. He began his career as a theater actor in his hometown and also undertook tours to other cities. In 1985 Dempsey made his film debut in the comedy Heaven Help Us. This was promptly followed by roles in Meatballs III: Summer Job and 1986 in Walt Disney’s Wonderful World of Color: A Fighting Choice, both in 1986, and he also received regular roles in the short-lived sitcom Fast Times (1986). Dempsey’s up-and-coming acting career received a major boost when he got a lead role in the 1985 film In the Mood.

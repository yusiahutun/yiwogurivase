Lifestyle

Multi-millionaire artist Frank Miller lives a luxurious lifestyle with a net worth of $25 million.

On January 27, 1957, Miller was born in Olney, Maryland.

During his childhood, Miller was a big fan of comic books. Graphic entertainment, cinema, and detective novels piqued his interest.

Former Miller executive coordinator Joanna Gallardo-Mills filed a complaint against Frank and Kimberly in 2012, stating that Cox had made the workplace hostile.

“Cox smeared her work area with feces, smashed her printer with a hammer, tossed phones at her, and even put a used feminine hygiene pad by her desk,” according to Gallardo-Mills.

As a result, Frank fired her when she complained about Kimberly’s behavior of her.

Producer Stephen L’Heureux, who collaborated on “Sin City: A Dame to Kill For,” sued Miller and fellow creator Silenn Thomas for $25 million in libel and economic interference in July 2020.

Stephen claimed the pair made false, misleading, and defamatory statements about his ownership of the development rights to “Sin City” and “Hard-Boiled.”

Frank Miller | Comics, Investment, and Endorsement

Comics

Miller authored the DC Comics mini-series “Ronin” in 1983, and DC honored him two years later in their “Fifty Who Made DC Great” collection.

He and illustrator David collaborated on “Batman: Year One” in 1987, which was converted into a 2011 animated picture.

Miller left DC Comics as they proposed a star rating system, which he saw as censorship, and moved to Dark Horse Comics, an indie publisher.

He and illustrator Dave Gibbons collaborated on the mini-series “Hard-Boiled” and “Give Me Liberty” in 1990.

Frank also created the 1992 comic book saga “RoboCop Versus The Terminator” and the screenplays for “RoboCop 2” and “RoboCop 3.”

His 1995 mini-series, “Big Guy and Rusty the Boy Robot,” was adapted into a Fox Kids cartoon movie in 1999.

Frank returned to DC Comics in the early 2000s, creating the mini-series “Batman: The Dark Knight Strikes Again” in 2001.

Imagine coming home to the terrible sight of your entire family murdered. That is what David Camm couldn't fathom on September 28, 2000, around 9:20 am. It took the effort and creativity of Bill Clutter, who worked on an animated crime scene reconstruction, to convince the jury that their client was indeed innocent. Now that he is out of prison, people wonder about his whereabouts and the actual killer.

Who is David Camm?

David Camm gained popularity in the early 2000s when he was accused of murdering his family. He was born on March 23, 1964, in Floyd County, Indiana, the United States of America. This means that he is 58 years old as of 2022.

David is a former state trooper of the Indiana State Police. His contentious murder made him famous.

What happened to the Camm Family?

What happened to Jill from the Camm family? David's testimony (which detectives first couldn't believe) recounts what transpired that fateful day. According to him, he returned to his Georgetown, Indiana home after basketball practice.

He was welcomed with a horrifying scene of his entire family murdered. His wife, Kim, was on the garage's floor, half-naked and lying in a pool of blood. David's children, five-year-old daughter Jill and seven-year-old son Bradley were slumped inside the SUV. His wife and children were shot execution-style.

Also, David thought that his son might still be alive and pulled him out of the SUV and tried to do CPR. When police officers arrived, they found Bradley lying on the floor on top of a prison-issue sweatshirt with DNA from two unknown individuals.

The first theory of the crime

Police started investigating the case and came up with different findings. The case faced numerous hiccups at the beginning with several false leads.

The first theory of the case was that David Camm came home after basketball practice and shot his family. They also stated that he tried cleaning up but stopped and called the Sellersburg State Police post for assistance.

Also, evidence suggested that David Camm had called from his residence at 7:19 pm on the evening of the murder. This was contrary to his statement that mentioned that at the time, he was playing basketball at the church (7:pm to 9:30 pm).

Another thing that made David Camm a prime suspect for the murder of his family is that he had a history of infidelity, which is often considered a motive for such murders.

However, the evidence considered vital proved to be erroneous as the case progressed. First, the call was made at 6:19 pm, not 7:19, as the investigators indicated earlier. Also, the time of death for the victims based on autopsies was indicated to be around 8:00 pm, earlier than the time specified of 9:30 pm.

Also, the evidence provided by the blood spatter analyst proved to be inaccurate. The DNA evidence at the scene also did not provide any matches.

The second theory of the Camm family murders

After investigators found out that the murder happened an hour earlier, they changed their story. But, of course, since David Camm was not at home, he had an alibi, and eleven people testified that they were with him during basketball practice from 7:00 pm until 9:00 pm.

David Camm’s first trial

In the beginning, investigators concentrated on the evidence on the crime scene and came up with different theories to prove that David Camm was guilty. David's first trial for the Camm family murders started in 2002 on January 14.

After deliberating for three days, the jury came up with a guilty verdict on March 17, 2002. And David was sentenced to 195 years in prison.

David’s second trial

David’s case did not end with the first trial. David Camm’s lawyer filed an appeal in August 2004, and the Indiana Court of Appeals overturned the conviction. The judge claimed that the testimony of women involved with David gave the jury a biased view.

Third trial

David was not content with the case's progress and got another trial after the Indiana Supreme Court reversed his conviction because of speculative evidence. And his third trial started in October 2012.

This time, David was fortunate enough to get a not guilty verdict and absolved him of all the crimes he was accused of. As a result, he was released from prison on October 24, 2013, thirteen years after he first went to prison.

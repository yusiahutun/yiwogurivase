Ali Bouzari On Snack Vs Chef

Snack vs. Chef sees a group of experienced real-life chefs and bakers re-creating all of the staples of the viewers’ childhood pantries. As for the judges’ panel including Ali Bouzari, their job is to know how to weed out the best of the best.

This show’s winner at the end of the season is awarded $50,000 and the title of the “best snacker”. The winner is determined on the basis of their ability to crack the code for how to make your favorite convenience-store junk-food items that may or may not be terrible for your health. So, clearly, Ali and his judge mates have a pretty crucial role to play.

Ali may already have some prior experience being a judge in reality-competition cooking shows as he also featured as one on Iron Chef Gauntlet in 2017. As an author, ingredient, and culinary scientist he also appeared in a total of 3 episodes of the TV series Food: Fact or Fiction?

How Much Is Ali Bouzari Net Worth?

Ali Bouzari, with a Ph.D. in Food Biochemistry, reportedly had more than $2 million net worth as of 2022.

He is a culinary scientist and over the years, he has collaborated with the likes of the Thomas Keller Group, Bar Tartine, and Benu.

The year 2014 saw him launch Pilot R+D, a consultancy and food think tank that he claims will help everyone from high-profile chefs to restaurant start-ups develop new cooking techniques and refine their food. Pilot R&D is a food innovation and development company founded by chefs who have built and run the research and development labs of some of the world’s best restaurants.

Ali also wrote a book called Ingredient: Unveiling the Essential Elements of Food. This very book won the IACP award for Best Reference Book.

He also has helped several universities including the Culinary Institute of America design their curriculum.

Other than Forbes, Ali has also been featured on Zagat’s 30 Under 30 lists, for his contribution to Popular Science, Eater, and WIRED, as a columnist for the San Francisco Chronicle, and as a guest expert on NPR, TEDx, TODAY, and Iron Chef America.

The culinary artist comes with the educational background of a B.S. in Biochemistry from the University of Texas and a Ph.D. from the University of California.
